Spatial Voice (Beta)

What is Spatial Voice (Beta)?

Spatial Voice (Beta) is a feature that allows users to engage in voice chat within spatial voice experiences on Roblox. As this feature is in beta testing, it is currently only available to eligible developers aged 13+. Eligible developers must enable the Spatial Voice (Beta) by agreeing to the use of their mic to collect and share their voice as part of this beta test.

Users who are not part of this beta test will have no change to their experience, and will not be able to hear or speak with other users via Spatial Voice (Beta) at this time. Please read the information below to find out when Spatial Voice (Beta) may be available for you in the future.

How do I enable or participate in Spatial Voice (Beta)?

While Spatial Voice (Beta) is in its initial testing phase, eligible users will receive a Private Message (PM) on their Roblox account with information on how to participate in Spatial Voice (Beta). Initially, Spatial Voice (Beta) will only be available in the “Roblox Community Space” experience. Developers will eventually be able to enable Spatial Voice (Beta) in their own experiences. At that time, eligible users in the Spatial Voice (Beta) will receive a notification when they join an experience that has enabled voice chat.

Roblox and our Customer Support team cannot add users by request. Please watch for future announcements to see when Spatial Voice may be available for you.

Eligible users are encouraged to enjoy this new and exciting way to communicate on Roblox, but must also continue to adhere to the Terms of Use and Community Standards as they would within any Roblox experience. Users that violate these rules may be suspended or removed from Spatial Voice (Beta) and have further moderation actions taken against their Roblox account. Roblox also reserves the right to remove users from the Spatial Voice (Beta) at any time at our discretion.

How can I tell who has Spatial Voice (Beta)?

While Spatial Voice (Beta) is in its current testing phase, eligible users in an experience will see a microphone icon next to the user name and above the avatar of other users that have voice chat enabled. Users will not otherwise be able to see who does or does not have Spatial Voice (Beta).

How to Mute, Block, or Report Abuse for Spatial Voice (Beta)

Muting another user’s voice chat

You can mute another user in a Spatial Voice (Beta) experience by clicking the microphone icon that will appear over their avatar’s head. Mutes only apply to voice chat and not text chat and will only last while you are in that game session.

Blocking another user

You can block another user in a Spatial Voice (Beta) experience to prevent them from engaging with you in voice chat or text chat. Please see our help article here: How to Block Another User .

Report Abuse

If someone is violating the Terms of Use or Community Guidelines, use the Report Abuse feature to file a report with our moderation team. Please see our help article here: How to Report Rule Violations .

For more information on Roblox safety features, please see our help articles here: Roblox Safety .

How can I turn off Spatial Voice (Beta)?

To disable the Spatial Voice (Beta) feature, follow the instructions below:

  • Go to Account Settings
  • Select the Privacy Tab
  • Toggle the Spatial Voice selector to OFF
    • The toggle will turn from green to grey, indicating that voice chat has been disabled for your account.
    • While you are in the Spatial Voice (Beta) you can toggle it back on at any time to re-enable voice chat.
